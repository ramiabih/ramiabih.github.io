# Say it out loud

I'm sitting here wondering what to write, I feel like writing but I really don't have much to say today. So I'm just going to write out what's in my head.

Do you think you are your body or do you have a body? I always thought I have a body and I need to take care of it like some kind of car.

Except the car can like kick you around and stuff and you're always in it so you do need to be good to it.

Lately though I think maybe we are our bodies, brain included, which means I should really take care of myself more. Although I probably should either way.

I think I feel a split because I live in my head. I mean don't we all do that? I'm back home in Lebanon this month and I keep hearing stories of people who knew me, or people who have fond memories of me, and for me that's so foreign.

What do you mean I exist outside my own thoughts?

I guess there are also random people I think about, but it's really odd when it's received. Apparently a lot of people follow me on LinkedIn (I wish they'd comment though).

In that case for them, I am all of me. For me, I am my thoughts.

Okay that's philosophical enough.

There are these older Lebanese women around me right now talking about sex, one of them pointed towards me and asked their friend if they would date younger men. Weird.

These women are like breaking my worldview, they talk about church and like such relatively prudish Lebanese women having the most raunchy conversations, it's hilarious. One's talking about trying to seduce their husband post-birth.

People are really funny. Yesterday night there was this very timid 20 year old barista at the same coffee shop. She never says a word, looks very normal, just like a normal 20 year old.

Last night she heard me playing my guitar and asked if she could use it, I gave it to her and she started shredding Master of Puppets on an acoustic guitar. It was glorious.

Then I woke up this morning and saw my dad still in bed on his phone. If you know my dad you would be absolutely shocked. He's 80 years old, runs up the stairs 2 at a time, hates sitting around, and is always out and about. It turns out he was watching the France Morocco game last night and was out till 4 AM, and wanted to be lazy in bed longer.

I lay down next to him and we both played mini games on our phones, it was very endearing.

You know, thinking about all this, I realise life can be pretty cool when you pay attention to it and stop living in your head.

Maybe it doesn't matter if you are your body or you have a body, maybe the important thing is to not wonder about that all the time and eavesdrop, interact with strangers, and find spontaneity with the people you love.

Or idk, it's kind of meta reflectively talking about not being reflective. I'm gonna go to a meeting now.
