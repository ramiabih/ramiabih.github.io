# Cougars and Master of Puppets

I'm sitting here wondering what to write, I feel like writing but I really don't have much to say today. So I'm just going to write out what's in my head.

Do you think you *are* your body or do you *have* a body? I always thought I am my thoughts, I have a body, and I need to take care of it like some kind of car.

Except the car can kick you around and you're always in it, so you do need to be good with it.

Lately, though, I think maybe we are our bodies (brain included). Which means I should really take care of myself more. Although I probably should either way.

I think I feel a split because I live in my head. I mean don't we all? I'm back home in Lebanon this month and I keep hearing stories of people who knew me, or people who have fond memories of me, and for me that's so foreign.

What do you mean I exist outside my own thoughts?

Maybe for them, I am my body.
But or me, I am only my thoughts. 

Okay that's philosophical enough.

----

I'm sitting at a cafe i've been spending a lot of time at. I love working from here, but I especially love people watching. They are really never what you expect.

There are these older Lebanese women around me right now who are relatively prudish Lebanese women. They talk about societal correctness, go to churn, gossip, etc. They have deep eye shadow makeup, are overly aware of their posture, and are fighting their age. 

Today however, they are *raunchy*. Talking about sex. One's talking about trying to seduce their husband post-birth with a robe. Then they complain how men don't like pregnancy sex. They keep looking around to make sure no-one is listening (I pretend i'm writing and I'm not interacting). One just pointed towards me and asked their friend if they would date younger men. I'm flattered. 

There is also this very timid young barista that works here in the evenings. She's just graduating school, never has much to say, and takes every oppurunity to text on her phone. Very normal 20 something year old. Doesn't take up any space. 

But then last night, she heard me playing my guitar and asked if she could use it, I gave it to her and she started shredding Master of Puppets on an acoustic guitar. It was glorious. She's playing an extremely hard metal song that came out before she was easy born, and really I caught my self misdjudging her. She finished then simply gave it back and went back to work. I was bewildered. 

Then I went home and I woke up this morning and saw my dad still in bed on his phone. If you know my dad you would be absolutely shocked. He's 80 years old, runs up the stairs 2 steps at a time, hates sitting around, and is always out and about. It turns out he was watching the France Morocco game last night and was out till 4 AM, and wanted to be lazy in bed longer. 

I asked him if he feelsl like having coffee, he said hes barely slept and he wants to be in bed. I wanted to spend some time with him before going to that weird cafe agan, so I just I layed down next to him and we both played mini games on our phones, it was very endearing.

------

You know, thinking about all this, I realise life can be pretty cool when you pay attention to it and stop living in your head.

Maybe it doesn't matter if you are your body or you have a body, maybe the important thing is to not wonder about that all the time and eavesdrop, interact with strangers, and find spontaneity with the people you love.

But who knows. it's abit too meta reflectively talking about not being reflective.
