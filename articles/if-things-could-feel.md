Practicing my similes and litany

I love you like things that would not become themselves without you
like the mug loves the coffee that warms it
like the canvas loves the paint that wakes it
like the seed loves the rain that splits it open
like the clay loves the fire that makes it hold

----------

I love you like things that only exist together
like the dream loves the sleep that lets it run
like the breath loves the chest it calms
like the story loves the voice that speaks it
like the instrument loves the hand that makes it ring

----------

I love you like desires that arrive unannounced
like a child craves love the moment they fall
like a woman craves safety alone in the dark
like anxiety craves a certainty it can't name
like grief craves a company it won't ask for
like pain craves the relief it can already feel
like hunger craves the morning
like rest craves the afternoon
like lust craves the evening

-----------

I love you like the world I built for myself
like the oud I play badly and love anyway
like my old runs through Fort Greene
like the table I built with my own hands, and the benches to match
like the knives I keep sharp for the sushi I make
like my chess club, my first office, my unread books
like a long walk with nowhere to be
